THE LOUDEST ANIMAL IN THE WORLD - Balaenoptera physalus

Balaenoptera physalus(Mammalia) - or we can call the fin whale
The fin whale is a cetacean belonging to the parvorder of baleen whales. It is the second-largest species on Earth after the blue whale.The largest reportedly grow to 27.3 m (89.6 ft) long with a maximum confirmed length of 25.9 m (85 ft), a maximum recorded weight of nearly 74 tonnes (73 long tons; 82 short tons), and a maximum estimated weight of around 114 tonnes (112 long tons; 126 short tons).

IT'S VOICE
Male fin whales emit a murmur, a cry that can be heard hundreds of kilometers away and is louder than the sound of an Airbus plane taking off if we stand right next to it. It's a way for males to communicate with females. Singing lasts about 7-15 minutes and contains many different combinations, each lasting from 7-26 seconds. The noise of ships interferes with communication between them and even affects the reproduction of this species.

EXPERIMENT
When fin whale sounds were first recorded by US biologists, they didn't realize that these unusually loud, long, pure and regular sounds were made by whales. First, they investigated the possibility that the acoustics were caused by equipment malfunction, geophysical phenomena, or even as part of a Soviet scheme to detect enemy submarines. Finally, biologists proved that the sound is the call of a fin whale.
