1.Africa is the only continent located on all four hemispheres and also the only continent that has both the Equator and the Prime Meridian running through.

2.Canada is "home" to more than half of the world's natural lakes, with a total of 31,752 lakes, In addition, 9% of Canada's area is covered by freshwater.

3.China shares borders with 14 countries: India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Myanmar, Nepal, Bhutan, Laos, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Tajikistan, Russia, Vietnam, Mongolia and North Korea.

4.Technically, the tallest mountain in the world is Mauna Kea in Hawaii, notMount Everest . This is because Everest is 8,850 meters above sea level, while the highest peak of Mauna Kea is only 4,205 meters above sea level. However, Mauna Kea is about 6,000 meters below sea level and its total height is 10,210 meters.

5.The Pacific island of Nauru is the only country without an official capital. The Nauru flag represents the island's location. The horizontal yellow line represents the Equator and the white star below it is Nauru
